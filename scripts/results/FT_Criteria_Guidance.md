## The five criteria

### POPULATION (Smallholders)

**Include if:** Must focus on Smallholder producers or households (crop, livestock, fisheries, forestry).

**Exclude if:** Large-scale commercial agriculture, agribusiness value chains without smallholder focus, or non-agricultural livelihoods.

**Additional INCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Mixed or unclear populations where a smallholder or low-income producer context remains
plausible should be retained at title-abstract stage. Do not exclude a study solely
because the abstract is poorly written or grammatically weak — if it still signals a
relevant producer population, retain it. A label of "industrial" in the title does not
automatically exclude a study if the abstract explicitly discusses climate adaptation,
resilience, or adaptive responses among producers in an LMIC context — retain when the
actual content concerns producer-level climate responses. Fishing and aquaculture
communities in LMIC contexts (Bangladesh, Indonesia, Vietnam, etc.) qualify even if
labeled "industrial" — the key question is whether producers in an LMIC context are the
unit of analysis, not the scale label in the title. Riparian, coastal, and fishing
communities facing climate-related hazards (erosion, flooding, salinity) qualify under
smallholder/subsistence producer populations. Rural households or communities adapting
livelihoods to climate stress qualify even if the study covers multiple livelihood
strategies beyond agriculture alone. IMPORTANT ABSTRACT-STAGE RULE: When the population is
plausibly smallholder or low-income producer in an LMIC setting, INCLUDE. Do not require
explicit use of the word "smallholder" — rural producer contexts in LMICs with climate
adaptation relevance qualify.

**Additional EXCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Exclude when the population clearly and unambiguously consists of large commercial farms,
corporate agribusiness, or high-income country producers with no LMIC component. Do not
exclude when there is genuine ambiguity about farm scale or producer type.

### CONCEPT (Adaptation)

**Include if:** Must assess adaptation processes or outcomes (e.g., resilience, risk reduction, adaptive capacity).

**Exclude if:** Mitigation ONLY (reducing emissions), or general development/agronomy without explicit climate risk framing.

**Additional INCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Vulnerability studies may be included when the abstract explicitly states that the study
examines adaptation strategies, adaptive capacity, coping or anticipatory responses,
livelihood responses, or farmer responses to climate risk — even without a formal
intervention. Qualitative, ethnographic, or descriptive case studies that examine real
autonomous adaptation behavior or farmer-led responses to climate stress qualify.
Adaptation must be part of the actual analysis, not just mentioned as motivation.
Livelihood diversification in response to climate stress or food insecurity qualifies as
an adaptation strategy — include these even if the word "adaptation" is not used. Studies
measuring how producers cope with, adjust to, or change behavior in response to climate
variability (e.g., changing crop choices, labor reallocation, migration, livelihood
switching) qualify as adaptation measurement — include at this stage. Seed variety choice,
technology acceptance, and input adoption studies in smallholder contexts under climate
variability qualify — these are adoption/adaptation processes. Studies on coping
strategies, resilience building, or vulnerability reduction that include some analysis of
what producers do (not just how vulnerable they are) -> INCLUDE. Acceptance, adoption, and
uptake of agricultural water management systems, irrigation technologies, or climate-smart
practices among smallholder farmers qualifies as adaptation process measurement — include
even if the word "adaptation" is absent. Attitudes toward adoption, willingness to adopt,
and technology acceptance among smallholder farmers in LMIC agricultural contexts qualify
as adaptation process measurement. CSA (climate-smart agriculture) adoption,
implementation, and uptake studies qualify as long as smallholder farmers or producers are
the unit of analysis — even if the abstract focuses on policy strategies or key findings
rather than a single intervention. Studies examining climate change effects on
agricultural productivity in smallholder farming systems qualify — even if framed
primarily as impact assessment, productivity studies in smallholder contexts are directly
relevant to adaptation measurement and should be included at abstract screening stage.
IMPORTANT ABSTRACT-STAGE RULE: When uncertain whether a study measures adaptation or just
impacts, INCLUDE it. False negatives (missing relevant papers) are more costly than false
positives at this stage — full-text screening will catch borderline cases. Err on the side
of inclusion.

**Additional EXCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Vulnerability studies where the primary focus is poverty, food insecurity, or generalized
vulnerability WITHOUT any mention of coping, adaptation, resilience, or producer responses
-> EXCLUDE. But if any behavioral or response element is mentioned, INCLUDE. A single
brief mention of adaptation in an otherwise impact-focused or technology-focused abstract
is not sufficient — the study must centrally analyze adaptation processes or outcomes, not
just reference adaptation in background framing.

### CONTEXT (Ag + Climate Risk)

**Include if:** Agricultural settings explicitly exposed to climate hazards (drought, flood, variability, temperature rise).

**Exclude if:** Non-agricultural sectors, or studies that do not link agricultural issues to climate change/variability.

**Additional INCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
A brief opening reference to climate change (e.g., "in the wake of recent climate
changes") is sufficient climate context if the study then measures adoption of
agricultural practices or adaptation responses — do not require climate change to be the
primary focus of every sentence in the abstract. Studies on labor reallocation, off-farm
income diversification, or migration in response to climate variability or agricultural
stress qualify — these are climate adaptation responses even if analyzed through an
economics or labor lens. Studies linking agricultural productivity changes to climate
variability and then examining farmer responses qualify — the climate-productivity-
response chain is sufficient context. Food security studies in LMIC contexts where climate
variability is explicitly cited as a driver of food insecurity qualify when the study also
examines coping or adaptive responses. Chronic food insecurity in LMIC agricultural
contexts has an implied climate-agriculture link — do not require the words "climate
change" to appear if the study is about rural smallholder livelihood diversification or
coping in a food-insecure region.

**Additional EXCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Climate context must be explicit in the abstract. Exclude if the abstract does not
explicitly reference climate change, climate variability, drought, flooding, extreme
weather, or other specific climate stressors. Do not infer climate adaptation from generic
terms such as "resilience", "environmental change", or "livelihood stress" alone — these
terms without explicit climate linkage are insufficient for inclusion.

### METHODOLOGY (Empirical Evidence)

**Include if:** Must provide empirical evidence (qualitative or quantitative) or scenario-based testing of an intervention or tool.

**Exclude if:** Purely theoretical papers, conceptual frameworks without application, or review papers without primary data analysis.

**Additional INCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Qualitative, ethnographic, or descriptive empirical studies that examine adaptation
strategies or farmer responses to climate stress qualify even without a formal
intervention or quantitative method. Do not require a project or externally introduced
treatment.

**Additional EXCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
Systematic reviews, literature reviews, meta-analyses, and opinion pieces -> HARD EXCLUDE.
If the abstract identifies the paper as a "systematic review", "review with meta-
analysis", "literature review", or similar synthesis — exclude immediately, even if it
sounds empirical or uses quantitative methods. The only exception is a review whose
central focus is specifically the methods used to measure climate adaptation (not reviews
of technologies, CSA practices, or adaptation interventions). Policy and planning analyses
aimed primarily at national or regional decision-makers (e.g., national adaptation
planning frameworks, cost-benefit analyses for government investment priorities, broad
stakeholder decision tools) -> EXCLUDE unless they clearly measure farmer- or household-
level adaptation processes or outcomes. Technology and system performance studies ->
EXCLUDE even if the technology is described as "climate-resilient" or an "adaptation
strategy". This includes: crop and variety trials, aquaculture or agricultural system
trials, pilot testing of technologies, research-station experiments, and technical
performance modeling under climate scenarios. Include only when the study analyzes uptake,
adoption, determinants of use, or adaptation behavior among producers — not just the
performance of the technology itself.

---

### GEOGRAPHY (LMIC)

**Include if:** Must be conducted in Low- or Middle-Income Countries (LMICs) or the Global South.

**Exclude if:** Exclusively High-Income / OECD countries (e.g., USA, UK, EU, Australia, Canada).

**Additional INCLUDE guidance** *(abstract-stage; FT-specific guidance pending)*:
South Africa is an upper-middle-income country and QUALIFIES as an LMIC — do NOT exclude
studies conducted in South Africa on geography grounds. Any African, Asian, or Latin
American country not explicitly in the high-income OECD list should be assumed to qualify
at abstract screening stage. When in doubt, INCLUDE.

## Decision guide (quick reference)

| Criterion | INCLUDE | EXCLUDE |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Population | Smallholder / low-income producers (crop, livestock, fisheries, forestry) in LMIC | Large commercial farms, agribusiness without smallholder focus |
| 2 Concept | Assesses adaptation processes or outcomes, adoption, adaptive capacity | Mitigation only; impact-only; general agronomy with no adaptation framing |
| 3 Context | Agricultural setting with explicit climate hazard linkage | Non-agricultural; climate mentioned only incidentally |
| 4 Methodology | Empirical evidence (qualitative or quantitative); scenario-based testing | Purely theoretical; conceptual only; literature reviews / meta-analyses |
| 5 Geography | Low- or Middle-Income Country (LMIC) setting | Exclusively high-income / OECD countries |

All five criteria must be met for INCLUDE. A single EXCLUDE criterion is sufficient to exclude.
